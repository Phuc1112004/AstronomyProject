Skyline and sparkling images from the space station:

The astronauts of the International Space Station (ISS) have a habit of launching some real photos out of the world that captivate enthusiasts. And more recently, they've come back with the ISS sharing a rare sight—the Earth's skyline at night—that fascinates people. The urban landscape and twinkling lights from outer space may no longer come as a surprise, with recent photos showing the horizon blooming at night. The posts were shared on the ISS's official Twitter page. The agency captioned: "Earth's skyline at night is surrounded by air light surrounded by stars in photographs taken from this station.

Image taken from iss space touch

Besides artificial light on the Earth's surface, the photos also capture the beauty of the natural light ring that surrounds the planet against a starry sky from space. The ISS also attached a redirect link to NASA Johnson's gallery on Flickr for more images, along with intricate details about the locations taken. The images taken by the crew show the city lights of the Netherlands, England, France and Belgium. Perhaps the most interesting image taken by ISS astronauts for Indian enthusiasts is when the ISS is hovering over Afghanistan. The photo not only shows the light of India and neighboring Pakistan, but also captures the well-lit border separating the two countries. NASA and the ISS actively share fascinating astronomical-themed photos from their various social media outlets over time, offering glimpses of Earth and outer space from their unique perspectives. Recent photos depicting a "cosmic rose" and a black hole "tsunami" have sparked curiosity and curiosity about these cosmic events. The post shared by the ISS has garnered nearly 120,000 likes on Instagram, while the Twitter post has been reposted several thousand times. It seems that one can hardly resist the beauty of the Earth glowing in the aura of the stars.
